Data Science with R for the Life Sciences (BIOL 806)

Dr. Easton R White

Fall 2021

University of New Hampshire





Welcome to the Data Science with R for the Life Sciences (BIOL 806) at the University of New Hampshire. This course is designed to introduce students to the basic data analysis and programming tools commonly used throughout the life sciences. Students will become proficient in R programming, data wrangling and cleaning, the principles of open and reproducible science, SQL database management, version control via Git/Github, building maps, and Bash command lines. The Schedule tab lists the topics covered and links to lecture material, assignments, and required readings.

All the materials for this course are open-source on Github. Please drop Easton an email (Easton.White@uvm.edu) if you are interested in using the course materials.

Note on COVID-19: UVM is currently preparing for a combination of in-person and remote instruction in the Fall. Although the situation might change, this course will be offered as a hybrid. We will meet once a week in person (or online synchronously) and the other two days of instruction will be completely online.
